MCC: A Stunning Political Debate in Nepal

Answer the following questions.
a. How does the poet describe the morning sun in the first stanza?

In the first stanza of the poem the poet described the morning sun similar to ribbon by making the surrounding purple in color.

b. What does the line "The news like squirrels ran" mean?

The news like squirrels ran means the rays or light of the sun travels so quickly by glowing all the areas at once.

c. What do you understand by the line "The hills untied their bonnets"?

"The hills untied their bonnets." it means that the hill at night time becomes cold and the heat of the sun makes them warm so here the hills are welcoming the sunrise to give them warmth.

d. Is the speaker watching the morning sun? Why? Why not?

There is no fix, according to the poem, whether the speaker is viewing the sun or not at that moment. However, the speaker is telling his or her personal experience with the sun since the speaker is viewing the change in colors of the steeple into amethyst unbound bonnets of the hill and he is also hearing to the singing of the beautiful bobolinks.

e. How does the sun set?

The Sun sets by sperading yellow color.

Reference to the context.
a. What, accourding to the speaker, is a day?

According to the speaker, a day is journey of people from life to earth where life is fixed and secure but death is uncertain.

b. What purpose does the hyphen is the first line serve in the poem?

A hyphen (_) is a type of punctuation mark used to connect words or sections of words. Emily Dickinson utilizes punctuation marks such as hyphens (_) in the poem because she cannot find suitable words to communicate her deep emotions.

c. What makes the poem lyrical and sonorous? Discuss.

The poem "A day" is a lyrical poetry in the sense that it expresses the speaker's own feelings or emotions via the use of alliteration, rhyme, symbolism, and simile.

d. Who are the target audience of the speaker? Why?

The speaker's intended audience is children since the children get up late in the morning. She learns this from the phrase "I will tell you, how the son rose." It also implies that these children may not have the opportunity to enjoy the beauty of the sun.
