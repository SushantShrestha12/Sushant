Answer the following questions.
a. How does the narrator describe the weather and its effects in the exposition of the story?

The narrator describes three days of constant rain, during which several crabs entered the house and their bad smell impacted the newly born child, causing the child to get ill.

b. Describe the strange old man as Palayo and his wife first encounter with in their courtyard.

Pelayo and his wife, Elisenda, discovered a strange elderly man in their yard dressed as a raga picker (a man who collects and sells rags). He had just a few pale hairs on his head and was in the horrible shape of a wet grandfather, his enormous barge wings half-fledged in the dirt and mud.

c. Why did Pelayo and Elisenda imprison the old man in the chicken coop?

Pelayo and Elisenda imprisoned the old man in the chicken coop because they believed the old man was an angle who came here to take their children life.

d. Why was Father Gonzaga not sure about the old man being a celestial messenger?

Father Gonzaga was sure about the old man being a celestial messenger because, according to him, a celestial messenger should be able to speak and understand Latin, but the old man is unable to do so.

e. Many people gathered at Palayo's house to see the strange man. Why do you think the crowd assembled to see him?

Many people gathered to Pelayo's house to see the strange old guy because the old man had enormous wings, something no one had ever seen before.

f. Some miracles happened while the crowd gathers to see the strange man. What are these miracles?

While the crowd gathered to view the strange man, the following miracles occurred:
(i) A blind guy who lost his sight but got three new teeth.
(ii) The paralytic who was unable to walk yet nearly won the lottery.
(iii) The leper whose spores sprouted into sunflowers.

g. State the irritating things that the people did with the strange old man.

The following are some of the irritating things that people did to the strange old man:
(i) People pulled his feather.
(ii) Stones were thrown at him in order to get him to stand.
(iii) They burned him after poking him with the branding iron.

h. How and why was the woman changed into a spider?

The women changed into spiders as a result of the lightning bolt of brimstone that came through the crack of the tow of the terrible thunderclap in the sky, and she was on her way to the dance without permission to dance and had disobeyed her parent.

h. Describe how Elisenda saw the strange man flying over the houses.

Elisenda was in the kitchen chopping vegetables for lunch when she noticed a gust of wind from the oceans and ran to the window, where she saw an angel making his first efforts at flight. He managed to keep his balance and make it through the remaining few homes, amazingly keeping himself up with the risky flapping of a senile vulture. She stared at him till she couldn't see him any more.
