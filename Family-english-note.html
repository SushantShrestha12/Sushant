B. Answer these questions.

a. What type of family is thought to be the oldest form of the family?

The nuclear family is thought to be the oldest form of family.

b. How does a family provide security to its member?

Family provides psychological security like warmth, love, and companionship and physical security like food, shelter, clothing, etc.

c. What were the features of medieval European families?

In medieval European families, the society was male-dominated and the heads of the clans were allowed to have several wives.

d. What caused the dissolution of extended family in the east?

Urbanization and industrialization cause many people to leave farms and went to urban centers to become industrial workers. This process led to the dissolution of many extended families.

e. What changes occurred in gender roles in the modern family that emerged after the Industrial Revolution?

Some of the gender roles in modern families that emerged after the industrial revolution are:-

  • Equality between sexes.
  • The family role once considered exclusively male or female broke down.

f. What is family law?

Family law is defined as the legal relationship among family members as well as the relationship between families and society at large.

g. How is modern marriage defined?

Modern marriage is defined as a voluntary union usually between a man and a woman.

h. What do special family courts try to do?

Special family courts try to attempt to deal more fairly with sensitive issues such as custody of children.

i. What does the legislation on child labor and child abuse declare?

This declares that the responsibility of society to accomplish the interests and desires of the children.

j. What is most common among most legal systems regarding property?

Most of the legal systems agree on the point regarding property i.e. distribution of the property depends on the interest of the decent.

Critical Thinking

We see many elderly people in the elderly homes these days in Nepal. Some of them are abandoned while others live there willingly. Do you think Nepali people are deviating from their traditional culture? Give reason.

Nepal is well-known for its traditional religion and culture. The elders carried down Nepalese culture from generation to generation. The elderly have a greater understanding and experience of Nepalese culture and tradition, whilst the youth do not. The old people of Nepal make many festivals possible, such as Dashain and Tihar. They have a better understanding of the rituals we perform in order to bring our celebration to an end using traditional culture.

Elderly people are the monuments of unaging intellects, and their experiences are the doorway to family behavior, yet in today's culture, many senior people live in elderly homes rather than with their offspring. As individuals get older, many people abandon their parents and send them to senior homes. Senior adults are not only abandoned by their offspring, but some of them choose to live in elderly homes because of their children's lack of care and affection. As a result, our country's culture is at risk, and it may be forgotten and not recognized. People, in my opinion, are departing from their traditional culture. Since there will be no one left who knows about traditional culture once all of the old have been placed in senior homes.

As a result, we must respect and care for our parents, even if they are elderly, so that they can pass on their cultural knowledge to us. If our culture is gone, our country may become unknown.
