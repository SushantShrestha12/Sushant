Find out the origin and the meaning of the following words from the text.

Penalty

Origin : Early 16th century from medieval latin word poenalitas.
Meaning : A disadvantage or handicap imposed on a player or team, typically for infringement of rules.

Major

Origin : From Latin word magnus which means 'great'
Meaning : A goal.

Stadium

Origin : Late Middle English from latin and greek word 'stadion'
Meaning : A sports arena with tiers of seats for spectators.

Trophy

Origin : Late 15th century from latin and greek word 'tropaion'.
Meaning : A cup or other decorative object awarded as a prize for a victory or success.

Defender

Origin : -
Meaning : A player whose task it is to protect their own side's goal.

Anthem

Origin : From late Latin word 'antiphona'
Meaning : A rousing or uplifting song identified with a particular group, body, or cause.

Dejection

Origin : Late Middle English from lation word 'deicere' which means throw down.
Meaning : A sad and depressed state.

Jubilant

Origin : From Latin in mid 17th century
Meaning : Feeling or expressing great happiness and triumph.

Record

Origin : From Old French.
Meaning : The best performance or most remarkable event of its kind.

Suave

Origin : Mid 19th century from latin word 'suavis' which means agreeable.
Meaning : charming, confident, and elegant.

Podium

Origin : mid 18th century via Latin from Greek podion
Meaning : A structure consisting of three adjacent platforms of different heights, on which the competitors finishing in first, second, and third places receive their awards.

Tournament

Origin : Anglo-Norman French variants of Old French word 'torneiement'
Meaning : A series of contests between a number of competitors, competing for an overall prize.

Reminiscent

Origin : Mid 18th century: from Latin word 'reminiscent' which means remembering
Meaning :Tending to remind one of something.

Incredible

Origin : From Latin word incredibilis.
Meaning : Difficult to believe; extraordinary.
